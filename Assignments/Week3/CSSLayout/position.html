Position

The position property allows one to make more complex layouts. They can be confusing. Below are some examples.

position:static

Position doesn't change even if values are added to top, bottom, right or left.

position:relative;
left:150px;

Position is relative to the container on the left and to where its normal position would be on the top.

position: relative;
top:50px;
left: 300px;

Position is relative to the container on the left and the yellow box on the top.

position: relative;
top: -200px;
left: 400px;

Position is relative to the container on the left and relative to where its "normal" position would be on the top.

position: fixed;
bottom:0px;
right:0;

Position is fixed in the viewport. Regardless of the size of the window, it always stays in the lower right corner.

position: absolute;
top: 200px;
right: 200px;

Position absolute looks like fixed but behaves releative to a positioned "ancestor" or the document body if it has no ancestor.
